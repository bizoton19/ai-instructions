About Recalls.gov

Recalls.gov is provided as a public service by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC), in cooperation with federal partner agencies. Each agency listed below maintains its own recall database. Select a category to visit that agency's official recall page.

Consumer Products

Recalls of household items, children's products, electronics, furniture, and other consumer goods regulated by the CPSC.

View Consumer Product Recalls

Motor Vehicles

Recalls of cars, trucks, motorcycles, tires, child safety seats, and other motor vehicle equipment regulated by NHTSA.

View Motor Vehicle Recalls

Boats

Recalls of recreational boats and associated marine equipment regulated by the U.S. Coast Guard Boating Safety Division.

View Boat Recalls

Food

Recalls of food products regulated by the FDA and USDA/FSIS, including meat, poultry, egg products, and packaged goods.

View Food Recalls

Medicine

Recalls of prescription drugs, over-the-counter medications, medical devices, and biologics regulated by the FDA.

View Medicine Recalls

Cosmetics

Safety alerts and recalls for cosmetics, personal care products, and related items regulated by the FDA.

View Cosmetics Recalls

Environmental Products

Recalls and safety actions for pesticides, chemicals, and other environmentally regulated products overseen by the EPA.

View Environmental Recalls

Report a Dangerous Product

If you believe a product poses an immediate safety risk, report it to the appropriate federal agency. Direct your report to the agency that regulates the type of product involved.

  • Consumer products: SaferProducts.gov — CPSC's incident reporting system
  • Vehicles, tires, child safety seats: NHTSA vehicle safety complaint portal
  • Food and drugs: FDA MedWatch
  • Meat, poultry, and eggs: USDA FSIS Meat and Poultry Hotline

Questions about this site?

Contact the Recalls.gov web team at webteam@cpsc.gov. For questions about a specific recall, contact the responsible agency directly.
