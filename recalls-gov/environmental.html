Environmental Product Recalls and Safety Actions

Recalls and safety actions for pesticides, herbicides, rodenticides, and other environmentally regulated consumer products are managed by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). The EPA also coordinates with CPSC on products that pose both consumer and environmental risks.

Dispose of recalled pesticides and chemicals safely. Do not pour recalled chemicals down drains or into trash without following the disposal instructions provided by the EPA or the recall notice. Contact your local household hazardous waste program for guidance.

EPA — Pesticide and Chemical Safety

The EPA's enforcement division handles recalls, stop-sale orders, and cancellations for pesticides, rodenticides, and other regulated chemical products sold to consumers. Visit the EPA for compliance and enforcement actions.

View EPA Enforcement Actions

EPA Pesticide Registration and Safety

All pesticides sold in the United States must be registered with the EPA. Find information on registered pesticides, label requirements, and safety restrictions. Contact the National Pesticide Information Center for additional guidance.

Visit EPA Pesticides Information

CPSC — Recalls Affecting Environment and Safety

Some consumer products pose both safety and environmental hazards. The CPSC coordinates with the EPA on recalls of products such as portable generators, gas-powered equipment, and chemical-containing consumer goods.
