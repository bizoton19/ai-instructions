Medicine Recalls and Drug Safety Alerts

Recalls of prescription drugs, over-the-counter medications, medical devices, and biological products are regulated by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). Visit the FDA's dedicated recall databases for each product category using the links below.

Do not stop taking a prescription medication without first consulting your doctor. If your medication is recalled, contact your pharmacist or physician for guidance on what to do. In most cases, alternative medications are available.

Drug Recalls

FDA drug recall database covering prescription drugs, over-the-counter medications, and compounded drugs. Includes recall class (I, II, or III) indicating the level of health hazard.

View Drug Recalls on FDA.gov

Medical Device Recalls

FDA medical device recalls cover a broad range of products including implants, diagnostic equipment, surgical tools, and in vitro diagnostic devices. Searchable by device name, manufacturer, and date.

View Medical Device Recalls

Report a Drug or Device Problem

MedWatch is the FDA's safety information and adverse event reporting program. Report serious problems with drugs, medical devices, biologics, or dietary supplements to the FDA.

Report on FDA MedWatch
