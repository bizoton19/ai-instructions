Boat Recalls

Recreational boat and marine equipment recalls are managed by the U.S. Coast Guard's Boating Safety Division. Manufacturers of boats, associated equipment, and flotation devices are required to notify the Coast Guard and owners of any defects that could create a substantial risk of personal injury or death.

Boat recalls are issued under the Federal Boat Safety Act. If your boat or marine equipment is recalled, the manufacturer is required to notify registered owners and provide a remedy at no cost to you.

U.S. Coast Guard Boating Safety

Search the Coast Guard's database of recalls for recreational boats, personal watercraft, inflatable flotation devices, and other marine equipment. Includes recalls dating back several decades.

Search Boat Recalls on uscgboating.org

Report a Boat Safety Defect

Boat manufacturers and distributors are required by law to report known defects to the Coast Guard. If you are aware of a defect that has not been reported, contact the Boating Safety Division.
