Motor Vehicle Recalls

Motor vehicle and equipment recalls are managed by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA). You can look up recalls by your vehicle's VIN, license plate, or by year, make, and model. NHTSA also manages recalls for car seats, tires, and motorcycle equipment.

Check your vehicle using your VIN number. Every vehicle sold in the United States has a unique 17-character Vehicle Identification Number. Your VIN is located on the lower left of your windshield, on the driver's side door jamb, and on your insurance card.

Vehicles, Tires, and Equipment

Search NHTSA's recall database by VIN, license plate, or vehicle year, make, and model. Includes recalls for cars, trucks, vans, motorcycles, tires, and motor vehicle equipment.

Search Vehicle Recalls on NHTSA.gov

Child Safety Seats

Look up recalls for infant carriers, convertible car seats, booster seats, and combination seats. Use the NHTSA car seat finder to check if a specific model is under recall.

Search Child Safety Seat Recalls

Report a Vehicle Safety Problem

If you've experienced a safety defect in a vehicle, tire, car seat, or motor vehicle equipment, file a complaint with NHTSA. Your report helps identify patterns that may lead to a recall.
